Sensation of Taste:
  1. Instruct the subject not to speak.
  2. Ask the subject to put their tongue out. Use cotton buds dipped in sugar (sweet), salt, vinegar (sour) and quinine (bitter) solutions apply them one at a time to the anterior 2/3rd of the tongue (near Macleod’s).
  3. Ask the subject to point to sweet, salt, sour and bitter on a card to indicate his response.
  4. The subject should rinse out his mouth with water between each test (Macleod’s).
