The lab data can be interpreted as follows:

  • Step 1: There is a decrease in the pH value indicating acidosis.
  • Step 2: It is respiratory acidosis.
  • Step 3: It is an acute case of acidosis.

For every 10 mmHg increase in pCO2, pH decreases by 0.08 pH unit and HCO3- increases by 6 mmol/L in acute cases]. In this case, as pCO2 has increased from 40 to 60 {change of 20mmHg} pH decreased from 7.4 to 7.24 {change of 0.08x2 =0.16 pH units} and HCO3- has slightly increased from 24 to 26 {change by 2mmol/L}. Therefore, it is a case of Acute Respiratory Acidosis.
