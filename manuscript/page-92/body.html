  2. Deflate the cuff slowly (2-3mmHg) until you hear a regular tapping sound (phase I Krotokoff sound).
  3. Record the reading to the nearest 2mmHg. This is the systolic pressure
  4. Continue to deflate the cuff slowly until the sounds disappear
  5. Record the pressure at which the sounds completely disappear as the diastolic pressure (phaseV).

Occasionally the muffled sounds persist (phase IV) and do not disappear, in which case the point of muffling is the best guide to the diastolic pressure.
