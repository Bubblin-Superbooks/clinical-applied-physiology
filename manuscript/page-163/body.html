Case no. 6

Hemorrhagic Shock.

A teenager came to the Emergency and Trauma Center at the AIIMS hospital after a road traffic accident that cut their tibial artery. They'd suffered a laceration wound on the left lower leg leading to blood loss of an estimated 1.6 liters, i.e., 30 % of the total blood volume. An attempt to control bleeding by applying pressure failed. 2 liters of Dextran was administered in the ambulance. On further examination, their pulse was measured @130/min, BP @84/61 mm Hg, and the Resp. Rate @24/min.
