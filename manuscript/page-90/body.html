1. Stand on the right side of the subject and explain the test procedure.
  2. Check for the normal functioning of stethoscope and place the earpieces comfortably into the ears along direction ears.
  3. Expose the subject chest completely
  4. Place the diaphragm stethoscope on any one of the heart artery (Mitral, Tricuspid, Pulmonary & Aortic) after proper counting of intercostal spaces.
  5. Palpation of the carotid artery provide a systolic time reference
  6. 1st Heart sound proceeds the carotid pulsation and 2nd heart sound follows the carotid pulsation
  7. Listen to one heart sound for several minutes before coming to any conclusion

JUGULAR VENOUS PRESSURE (JVP)

  1. Stand on the right side of the subject and explain the procedure
  2. Position the subject so that he is reclining supine comfortably until the wave form is clearly visible (start at 45 degree)
  3. Rest the patient head on pillow to ensure the neck muscles are relaxed
