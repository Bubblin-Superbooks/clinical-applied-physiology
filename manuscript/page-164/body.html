1. Define shock.
  2. Explain the Compensatory Mechanisms in play during a shock.
  3. What is the course of treatment?
Answers:
  1. Hemorrhagic Shock.
  2. Shock is a syndrome characterized by low cardiac output which is inadequate to maintain normal tissue perfusion. The types of shock are
    • Hypovolemic,
    • Hemorrhagic,
    • Traumatic,
    • Surgical,
    • and Dehydration Shock.
  3. There are two types of natural Compensatory Mechanisms that play a role during a shock.
    1. Rapid Compensatory Mechanisms, and
    2. Long-Term Compensatory Mechanisms.
