1. Why does the patient develop seizures and carpopedal spasm?
Answers:
  1. During radical thyroidectomy, parathyroid gland has also been removed. This leads to decrease in Parathormone and decrease in calcium.
  2. Serum Calcium level (normal 9-11mg%).

Other tests are:

  • Chvostek’s sign: Facial tics on tapping facial nerve at pre-auricular area.
  • Trousseau’s sign: Carpopedal spasm (Figure 6) on tying BP cuff at 100 mm Hg
  • Erb’s sign: Policeman tip hand
  • Laryngeal stridor: Life threatening condition, may require tracheostomy
