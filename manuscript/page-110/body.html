Facial nerve:
  1. Explain the test procedure to the subject.
  2. Watch for spontaneous or involuntary movements (Macleod’s).
  3. Look for facial symmetry, nasolabial folds, and furrows over the head.
  4. Look for eyebrow’s, angle of mouth and width of palpebral fissure.
  5. Ask the subject to bare their teeth, smile, whistling or blowing with lips tightly shut (Orbicularis Oris) (Hutchison’s).
  6. Ask the subject to raise their eyebrows and look for asymmetry of the forehead (Frontalis) (Hutchison’s).
  7. Ask the subject to screw their eyes shut while you try to prevent this with your finger and thumb (Orbicularis oculi) (Hutchison’s).
