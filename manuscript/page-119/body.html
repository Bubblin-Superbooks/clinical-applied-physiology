1. Map out the boundaries of any area of reduced, absent or increased sensation.
  2. For deep pain explain the test and ask the subject to report it as soon as he feels discomfort (Macleod’s).
  3. Squeeze the muscle bellies e.g. calf, biceps or apply pressure to finger nail or toenail beds.
  4. Do not apply with an instrument e.g. pen (Macleod’s).
  5. For temperature touch the subject with a cold metallic object e.g. tuning fork, and ask if it feels cold.
  6. More sensitive assessment requires tubes of hot and cold water at controlled temperatures.
  7. For vibration place a vibrating 128Hz tuning fork over the sternum and ask the subject “Do you feel it buzzing?”
