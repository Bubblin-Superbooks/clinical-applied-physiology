1. Map out the boundaries of any area of reduced, absent or increased sensation.
  2. For deep pain explain the test and ask the subject to report as soon as they feel unconfortable (Macleod’s).
  3. Squeeze the muscle bellies e.g. calf, biceps or apply pressure to finger nail or toenail beds.
  4. Do not apply pressure with an instrument or tool e.g. pen (Macleod’s).
  5. For temperature sensitivity, touch the subject with a cold metallic object e.g. tuning fork, and ask if they felt anything (cold?).
  6. More sensitive assessment requires tubes of hot and cold water at controlled temperatures.
  7. For vibration place a vibrating 128Hz tuning fork over the sternum and ask the subject “Do you feel it buzzing?”
  8. Next place it on the tip of the great toe, if sensation is impaired, place the fork on the interphalangeal joint and progress proximally—medial malleolus, tibial tuberosity and anterior
