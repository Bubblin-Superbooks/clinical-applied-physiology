1. nicotine Acetylcholine receptors (Nm). It is a rare but autoimmune disease. Neurotransmitter acetylcholine is destroyed in synaptic cleft by acetylcholinesterase. This produces a miniature end plate potential (MEPP). The subject suffers from profound weakness of muscles and he gets fatigued easily.
  2. It can be treated by administration of Neostigmine or other effective anticholinesterase inhibitors that help increase concentration of acetycholine in the cleft by preventing its destruction.
