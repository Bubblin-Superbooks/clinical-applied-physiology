• Hemophilia is congenital deficiency of Factor VIII: Hemophilia A Factor IX: Hemophilia B.

Hemophilia A is inherited as sex linked. The subject has a normal bleeding time but a prolonged clotting time. The patient is treated by Factor VIII rich preparation prepared from plasma during hemorrhage. The patient needs to be careful when travelling by two wheelers. Role of factor VIII is shown below in coagulation pathway.


Intrinsic:

XII to XII a occurs when collagen is exposed in blood vessels (in vivo). Or in-vitro when the blood comes in contact with negatively charged blood.


Extrinsic:

Injury to blood vessel or tissue causes activation of thromboplastin or factor III.
