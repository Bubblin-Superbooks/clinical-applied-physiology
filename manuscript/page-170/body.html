Intrinsic:

FXII to FXIIa (activated) occurs when collagen is exposed in the blood vessels (in vivo). Or in-vitro when the blood comes in contact with negatively charged blood.

In-vitro, Factor-XII can be activated by exposing blood plasma to certain artificial surfaces or substances like glass or kaolin. This mimics the physiological process of exposure to damaged blood vessel surfaces during injury.


Extrinsic:

Injury to blood vessel or tissue causes activation of thromboplastin or Factor-III.

  1. The patient is treated with Factor-VIII rich extract prepared from their plasma during hemorrhage. Desmopressin triggers Factor-VIII released from the endothelium. Factor-VIII rich plasma or cryo-precipitate or purified recombinant Factor-VIII may be given.
