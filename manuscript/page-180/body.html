Case Study

High-altitude Pulmonary Edema

or Acute Mountain Sickness.

A 60 year old man makes a pilgrimage to Kedarnath. They complain of dyspnea, headache, dizziness, and insomnia. They were short of breath even while in rest or sitting down. On examination, their fingernails showed slight cyanosis, and the Pulse Oximeter showed only 68% saturation.


Questions:
  1. What is your diagnosis?
  2. What is HAPE?
  3. Explain the physiological basis of the pilgrims symptoms due to high-altitude.
  4. Enumerate the changes that occur during acclimatisation at high-altitude?
