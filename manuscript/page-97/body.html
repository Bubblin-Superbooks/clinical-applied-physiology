1. Deflate the cuff slowly (2-3mmHg) until you hear a regular tapping sound (phase I Krotokoff sound).
  2. Record the reading to the nearest 2mmHg. This is the systolic pressure
  3. Continue to deflate the cuff slowly until the sounds disappear
  4. Record the pressure at which the sounds completely disappear as the diastolic pressure (phaseV). Occasionally muffled sounds persist (phase IV) and do not disappear in which case the point of muffling is best guide to the diastolic pressure
