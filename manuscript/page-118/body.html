Tactile Localisation & Discrimination.

Understanding pain, thermal sense, sense of vibrations, sense of stereognosis, and proprioception.

  1. Explain the test procedure and make the subject comfortable.
  2. For light touches: While subject looks away or closes his eyes, use a wisp of cotton wool and ask the subject to respond to each touch (Macleod’s).
  3. Time the stimuli irregularly and make a dabbing rather than a stroking or tickling stimulus (Macleod’s).
  4. Compare each side for symmetry.
  5. For superficial pain use a fresh neurological pin e.g neurotip and not the hypodermic needle. Dispose after every use (Macleod’s).
  6. Explain and demonstrate the ability to feel sharp pain. Pinprick at different test points and compare for symmetry (Macleod’s).
