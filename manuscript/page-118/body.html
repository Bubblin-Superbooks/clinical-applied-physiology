1. For light touch while subject looks away or closes his eyes, use a wisp of cotton wool and ask the subject to respond to each touch (Macleod’s)
  2. Time the stimuli irregularly and make a dabbing rather than a stroking or tickling stimulus (Macleod’s)
  3. Compare each side for symmetry
  4. For superficial pain use a fresh neurological pin e.g neurotip and not to use hypodermic needle, dispose it after every use (Macleod’s)
  5. Explain and demonstrate the ability to feel sharp pinprick at different test points and compare for symmetry (Macleod’s)
  6. Map out the boundaries of any area of reduced, absent or increased sensation (Macleod’s)
  7. For deep pain explain the test and ask the subject to report it as soon as he feels discomfort (Macleod’s)
  8. Squeeze the muscle bellies e.g. calf, biceps or apply pressure to finger nail or toenail beds.
  9. Do not apply with an instrument e.g. pen (Macleod’s)
  10. For temperature touch the subject with a cold metallic object e.g. tuning fork, and ask if it feels cold.
  11. More sensitive assessment requires tubes of hot and cold water at controlled temperatures (Macleod’s)
