A 35 year old has undergone radical thyroidectomy for papillary carcinoma of thyroid gland. Her recovery was uneventful till the second postoperative day, when suddenly she developed seizures and her voice was choked.

The doctors on duty could demonstrate carpopedal spasm. The patient recovered only after an i.v injection of calcium gluconate.

Questions:

  1. What was the problem in this case?
  2. What are biochemical tests done to confirm the diagnosis?
  3. Why does the patient develop seizures and carpopedal spasm?

  1. During radical thyroidectomy, parathyroid gland has also been removed. This leads to decrease in Parathormone and decrease in calcium.
