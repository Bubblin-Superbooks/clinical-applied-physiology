The ECG recording obtained is analyzed as follows:
  1. Rate – 1500/R-R in interval gives the heart rate.
  2. Rhythm – Look at the rhythm strip (long duration recording obtained from lead 2) and check for a constant PR interval.
  3. Sequence – Ensure that the ECG waves follow the sequential pattern of P wave appearing first, followed by the QRS complex and finally T wave, before the next P wave appears.
  4. Axis – Calculate the axis of the heart. Normal cardiac axis ranges from -30 degrees to +110 degrees. If the axis is less than -30 degrees, it is considered as left axis deviation and if the axis is greater than +110 degrees, it is considered right axis deviation.
