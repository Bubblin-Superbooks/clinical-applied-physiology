Question:

Interpret the lab data and write a diagnosis.

Answer:

The lab data can be interpreted as follows:

  • Step 1: Look at pH, which is lowered in this case.
  • Step 2: Is it respiratory or metabolic; since pCO2 is predominantly affected, it is a case of respiratory acidosis.
  • Step 3: Is it acute or chronic; it is a chronic case.

In chronic cases, for every 10mmHg rise in pCO2, the value of pH decreases by 0.03 units and the value of HCO3 increases by 4 mmol/L. In this case, pCO2 has increased from 40-60 (a change of 20mmHg), pH has decreased from 7.4 to 7.34 (a change of 0.06), and HCO3- increased from 24 to 32 (a change of 4x2=8mmol/L). Therefore, it is a case of Chronic Compensated Respiratory Acidosis.
