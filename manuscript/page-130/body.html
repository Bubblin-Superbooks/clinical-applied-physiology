SUPERFICIAL REFLEX ABDOMINAL REFLEX

  1. Make the patient lie supine and relaxed with the abdomen uncovered(Hutchison’s)
  2. A thin wooden stick or reverse end of the tendon hammer is stroked briskly but lightly in a medial direction across the upper and lower quadrants (Macleod’s)
  3. A ripple of contraction of the underlying muscles with the umbilicus moving laterally and up or down depending upon the quadrant test. (Macleod’s)

PLANTER REFLEX

  1. Make the subject comfortably seated on a chair or lie supine on the bed
  2. Explain the test procedure to the subject
  3. Stand on the subjects right side and completely relax the leg of the subject
  4. Gently scratch the outer edge of the subjects foot with a blunt object from heel towards the little toe and medially across the metatarsals(Ghai)
