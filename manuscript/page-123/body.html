Superficial Reflex Abdominal Reflex:
  1. Make the patient lie supine and relax with the abdomen uncovered.
  2. A thin wooden stick or reverse end of the tendon hammer is stroked briskly but lightly in a medial direction across the upper and lower quadrants.
  3. A ripple of contraction of the underlying muscles with the umbilicus moving laterally and up or down depending upon the quadrant test.
Planter reflex:
  1. Make the subject comfortably seated on a chair or lie supine on the bed.
  2. Explain the test procedure to the subject.
  3. Stand on the subjects right side and completely relax the leg of the subject.
