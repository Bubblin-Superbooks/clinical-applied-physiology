Cadiovascular System


Measuring the pulse:
  1. Stand on the subjects right side and explain the test procedure,
  2. Place the subjects forearm in the semipronated position with the wrist slightly flexed,
  3. Place pads of three middle fingers over the right radial artery (see picture overleaf),
  4. Count the radial pulse for full one minute only when it has resume normal rate,
  5. Assess rate rhythm character, volume and condition of vessel wall,
  6. Palpate both radial pulses simultaneously to assess volume difference,
  7. Palpate femoral and radial pulse simultaneously noting delay between two and any difference in volume between them.
