CENTRAL NERVOUS SYSTEM

CRANIAL NERVES HYPOGLOSSAL NERVE XII
  1. Explain the test procedure to the subject
  2. Ask the subject to open the mouth look at the tongue at rest for wasting, fasciculation’s or involuntary movements
  3. Ask the subject to protrude out his as far as possible
  4. Look for deviation or involuntary movements (Macleod’s)
  5. Ask the patient to move tongue from side to side
  6. Test power by asking the patient to press the tongue against the inside of each cheek in turn while you press from outside with your finger (Macleod’s)
  7. Assess speech by asking the patient to say YELLOW LORRY (Macleod’s)
  8. Assess swallowing with water swallowing movement (Macleod’s)
