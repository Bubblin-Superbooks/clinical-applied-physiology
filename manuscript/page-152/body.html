Case no. 1

Hypercholesterolemia.

A 15 year old slim and fit boy collapsed after completing a cross-country run at his school in the morning. He was rushed to the hospital emergency ward and pronounced dead by afternoon.


Family History:

The boy was an avid cricket player. His father has hypercholesterolemia. Upon autopsy, patient had lumps over knuckles, Xanthelasma present, and coronary arteries were completely blocked having a pin hole opening.


# Lab Data:
1. Total cholesterol: 290mg/dL
