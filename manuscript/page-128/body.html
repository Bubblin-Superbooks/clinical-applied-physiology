1. Explain the test and give proper instructions of the procedure to the subject.
  2. Make the subject completely relaxed with attention diverted.
  3. Use reinforcement whenever a reflex appears to be absent (jendrassik’s maneuver) (Macleod’s).
  4. Slightly dorsiflex the ankle so as to stretch the Achilles tendon and with your other hand strike the tendon on its posterior surface, or the sole of the foot (Hutchison’s).
  5. A quick contraction of the calf muscles results (Hutchison’s).
  6. Comparing each reflex with that of the other side, check for symmetry of response and ensure that both the limbs are positioned identically.
