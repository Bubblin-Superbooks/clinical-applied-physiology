Questions:
  • What is the site of lesion?
  • Which sensations are carried by the concerned sensory tract?
  • Name the test the doctor performed?
Answers:
  1. The site of lesion – fibers of dorsal column is the fasciculus gracilus and fasciculus cuneatus is effected by Tabes dorsalis. It is a neurological symptom of tertiary syphilis that affects degeneration of dorsal nerve root.
  2. Sensations carried by the dorsal tract can be memorized by the pneumonic PSVT Propioception Somatic localization (two point discrimination) Vibration Touch (fine).
  3. Rhomberg’s sign is positive in patients with Tabes dorsalis. This is a sign of sensory ataxia. On removing the visual cues the person falls. In cerebellar ataxia whether eyes are open or closed the person falls.
