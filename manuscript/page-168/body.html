Case Study

Hemophilia and Purpura.

A 5 year old boy has complaints of excessive bruises around pressure points of knees and elbows. The parents noted that he bleeds easily when the skin surface is scratched. His maternal grandmother has had a history of hemophilia.

On examination, the subject's platelets count was @2,50,000/mm3 (normal is 1,50,000-4,00,000/mm3), bleeding time was @6minutes (normal is 2-8min), factor-VIII level was low, and vWF levels was normal.
