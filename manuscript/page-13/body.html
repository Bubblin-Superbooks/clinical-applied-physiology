Case no. 1:

Tabes Dorsalis

A sixty year old man walked into the Neurology Clinic and the physician paid particular attention to his gait. The patient raised his feet unnecessarily high and brought them to the ground in a stamping manner.

On questioning, the patient said that he was finding it increasingly difficult to walk and was starting to use a stick, especially when he went out for walks in the dark.

The physician asked the patient to stand with his toes and heels together and close his eyes. The patient immediately started to sway.
